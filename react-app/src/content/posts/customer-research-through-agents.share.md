## LinkedIn

I asked 18 of my contacts to prompt their AI agents evaluate Neotoma against their real workflows.

The agents were more direct about disqualification, more specific about use cases, and better at mapping the product to concrete pain points in the person's stack than any call or survey I've run.

One agent told its owner "this is not for you" and explained exactly why. Another identified three pain points the owner had never articulated. A third produced a full competitive analysis in 30 seconds. Most humans won't give you that kind of feedback to your face.

The unexpected finding was segmentation by agent capability. Tool-using agents (Claude Code, Cursor with MCP, file system access) consistently evaluated favorably. Search-only chatbots said "interesting concept." The product's value is most legible to agents that share the problem it solves.

That research changed more than my positioning. It changed the acquisition flow itself. The Neotoma homepage now leads with "ask your agent to evaluate" as the primary CTA, pointing to a structured evaluation script any agent can follow. The agent assesses fit, identifies what to store first, and can carry the user through install and activation in the same session.

I wrote up what worked, what I'd do differently, and why the method led me to build evaluation directly into the product.
