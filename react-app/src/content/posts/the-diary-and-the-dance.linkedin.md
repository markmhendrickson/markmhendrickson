I've been thinking about a habit I want to cultivate more deliberately: revealing my inner world to the external one, and tuning into what comes back.

Rick Rubin calls it writing a diary. You don't need permission or approval to create. The act of expressing your inner experience is reason enough. But the diary is only half. The other half is the dance: the feedback loop between what you put out and what the world sends back.

This changed how I think about startups too. I used to believe in the standard lean startup model. Ship an MVP, wait for the verdict, pivot if needed. I don't think that's how it works. There is no static MVP. The MVP is a continuum. Every version is an MVP. The feedback is never binary. And the whole process is more art than science at the start.

Same with writing. I used to treat it as a formal event. Now I think it has to be part of daily life. No right moment. No required audience. Just the practice.
