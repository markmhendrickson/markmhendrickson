## LinkedIn — About (paste into About)

AI agents are getting good at tasks but bad at memory. They lose contacts between sessions, overwrite facts without history, and cannot explain what changed or why. The more you rely on them, the more you become the human sync layer — re-entering the same context across every tool.

I'm building Neotoma to fix that: a deterministic state layer for long-running agents. Every observation is versioned, every entity snapshot is reproducible, every decision can be replayed. Schema-bound, local-first, cross-platform via MCP, and entirely user-controlled. Your agents remember what matters, and you can prove they're right.

The principle underneath is the same one that's driven all of my work: people should control their own data, memory, money, and digital infrastructure — not cede it to platforms that optimize for engagement over truth.

I work as a solo founder in Barcelona. Every workflow — email, finance, content, product — runs through agents operating against a shared source of truth. That only works because the state layer is explicit and inspectable, which is exactly the contract Neotoma provides.

Previously: TechCrunch (writer/product), Plancast (co-founder, acquired by Active Network), KITE Solutions (co-founder), Hiro/Stacks (UX lead), Leather/Trust Machines (GM). Nearly two decades across consumer web, crypto, and startups. Full arc: https://markmhendrickson.com/timeline
